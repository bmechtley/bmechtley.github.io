I study wiggles and like to make and listen to noise and silence.

Here's a preview of an ongoing soundscape mapping project that allows users to scrub across a map of GPS paths to dynamically morph between field recordings. For some other sound maps, check out this list. For some information on acoustic ecology, check out the American Society for Acoustic Ecology.

I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Kansas Biological Survey at the University of Kansas, where I'm working on computational models of population ecology. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science with a concentration in Arts, Media, and Engineering from Arizona State University and am recovering from working on a thesis titled "Techniques for Soundscape Retrieval and Synthesis." I also worked a bit on interactive tools for search in environmental sound databases alongside some fine folks at the Centre for Digital Music.
